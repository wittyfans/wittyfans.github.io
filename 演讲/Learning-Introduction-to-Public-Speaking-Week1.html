What is rhetoric?

  • Rhetoric is a effective speech! For us?

  • It’s about analyzing the situation you find yourself in and then using rhetorical tools to craft a strategic and effective response

Rhetoric Situation

Complicating factors comprise the rhetoric situation. Good speaks analyze and respond to rhetoric situation correctly. Rhetoric situation include:

  • Topic,
  • Setting
  • Audience
  • Occasion
  • Creditability

This Rhetoric situation, this whole bunch of skills that developed through experience, and study, and practice, need you have a strong sense of judgement about what to do and when to do it. It’s about having the ability to judge rhetorical situations accurately, devise smart responses, and perform them well. Medium of sound,Medium of writing

Don’t forget that speaking and writing are different mediums

Lesson Summary

  1. Speaking and writing are different mediums
  2. Language is acquired. Writing is learned.
  3. Language evolved. Writing was invented.
  4. Language is ubiquitous. Writing is not.

Good delivery is easy to understand

To do a good delivery,Well, based on what we know now about how we process speech, We need to provide two really important cues to help listeners. Context and prosody

Context

When context is missing ,you brain might slow down, Especially when confronted with unexpected words, or different ideas.So we need give our audiences contextual cues.what am I talking about? What am I talking about it? And what am I going to be talking about next? Previewing you key points ,providing transition and reviews!

Prosody

Prosody is a ongoing stream of hints about how to efficiently process the language.

So?

As speakers, we operate in the medium of sound, we should strive to speak in a way that’s easy and pleasant for the audience to understand. Providing contextual and prosodic cues sounds good because that’s what listeners of language need.

Lesson Summary

  1. Listeners need cues to easily and quickly decode talk
  2. Contextual cues provide situational information.
  3. Prosodic cues(pitch, rate, etc) provide auditory information

Week1 Review

This week was really about overviewing the framework we’re going to take in this course. In essence, we take a rhetorical approach to the medium of speech with a special focus on linguistics. So to begin with, rhetoric is strategic communication. Good speakers use skills, experience, and judgement to analyze a situation and design an effective response. And the rhetorical canons can help us prepare that effective response. We have to understand the medium of speech. So, we certainly want to be using writing. But we have to remain focused on the oral message. And that requires understanding how language works. Knowing that listeners need cues to guide their interpretation.
